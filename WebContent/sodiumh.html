Why Sodium Hydroxide ?

  • Sodium hydroxide is used to hydrolyze tomato pomace, breaking down the cell walls and releasing lipids (fats) from the waste material.

  • Sodium Hydroxide in water is is more effective for the initial extraction from the pomace matrix.

  • It reacts with the pomace to convert it into a more usable form, which is then filtered to obtain a waxy lipid substance.

  • Utilizing sodium hydroxide for extracting lipids from tomato waste reduces reliance on petroleum-based chemicals like BPA, leading to a more sustainable and eco-friendly packaging solution.

  • The sodium hydroxide-derived resin provides effective protection against corrosion and maintains the integrity of the metal can, ensuring the safety and quality of the food inside.

  • The resultant coating does not contain BPA, addressing health concerns associated with traditional epoxy resins used in food packaging.
